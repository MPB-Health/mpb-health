# Medical Expense Tracker - Usage Instructions

## Overview

The MPB Health Medical Expense Tracker helps members organize and track their medical expenses for sharing submission. This system includes multiple formats to fit your workflow preferences.

## Available Formats

### 1. HTML/PDF Version (`medical-expense-tracker.html`)

**Best For:** Printing, digital viewing, or saving as PDF

**How to Use:**
1. Open `medical-expense-tracker.html` in any web browser
2. Print to PDF using your browser's print function (Ctrl+P / Cmd+P)
3. Save the PDF or print physical copies
4. Fill out by hand or type directly in the PDF

**To Create PDF:**
- **Chrome/Edge:** File → Print → Save as PDF
- **Safari:** File → Export as PDF
- **Firefox:** File → Print → Save to PDF

### 2. CSV/Excel Version (`medical-expense-tracker.csv`)

**Best For:** Digital tracking with formulas and calculations

**How to Use:**
1. Open `medical-expense-tracker.csv` in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers
2. Save a copy for each tracking period (monthly/quarterly)
3. Use spreadsheet formulas to auto-calculate totals
4. Sort and filter by date, provider, or status

**Recommended Formulas:**
- Total Billed: `=SUM(G2:G100)`
- Total Paid: `=SUM(I2:I100)`
- Applied to IUA: `=SUM(J2:J100)`
- Eligible for Sharing: `=SUM(K2:K100)`

## Tracking Your Medical Expenses

### What to Record

For each medical visit or service, document:

1. **Date of Service** - When you received care
2. **Provider Information** - Name, phone, address
3. **Type of Service** - Office visit, lab work, surgery, etc.
4. **Diagnosis/Reason** - Why you received care
5. **Financial Details:**
   - Total amount billed by provider
   - Any discounts negotiated
   - Amount you paid out of pocket
   - Amount applied to your IUA
   - Amount eligible for community sharing
6. **Pre-Authorization** - If required for major procedures
7. **Status** - Pending, Submitted, Under Review, Shared, Denied
8. **Notes** - Reference numbers, claim IDs, important details

### Understanding the Columns

**Total Billed Amount**
- The original amount on your medical bill before any adjustments

**Discount/Negotiation**
- Cash discounts, provider adjustments, or negotiated reductions
- Many providers offer 20-40% cash discounts

**Amount I Paid**
- What you actually paid to the provider
- Include co-pays, deposits, and full payments

**Applied to IUA**
- Portion that counts toward your Initial Unshareable Amount
- Track this carefully to know when you've met your IUA

**Amount for Sharing**
- Expenses eligible for community sharing after IUA is met
- Must meet sharing guidelines and have proper documentation

**Pre-Auth Number**
- Required for surgeries and procedures over $5,000
- Contact MPB Health before scheduled procedures

**Status Options**
- **Pending** - Need to submit
- **Submitted** - Sent to MPB Health for review
- **Under Review** - Being evaluated by sharing team
- **Approved** - Eligible and processing
- **Shared** - Payment sent to provider or member
- **Denied** - Not eligible (with reason)
- **More Info Needed** - Additional documentation required

## Required Documentation Checklist

Before submitting for sharing, ensure you have:

- ✅ Itemized medical bills (showing CPT codes and detailed charges)
- ✅ Proof of payment (receipts, credit card statements, EOB)
- ✅ Medical records or visit notes (for major procedures)
- ✅ Pre-authorization documentation (if required)
- ✅ Prescription documentation (for pharmacy claims)
- ✅ Lab/imaging results (if applicable)
- ✅ Completed MPB Health sharing request form

## Best Practices

### 1. Record Immediately
- Log expenses as soon as you receive care
- Don't wait until bills arrive
- Keep a running log throughout the year

### 2. Request Itemized Bills
- Always ask for detailed, itemized bills
- Standard bills often lack required detail
- Itemized bills show CPT codes needed for processing

### 3. Negotiate Costs
- Ask about cash discounts before paying
- Many providers offer 20-50% discounts for immediate payment
- Negotiate payment plans for large bills

### 4. Organize Documents
- Create a folder for each medical incident
- Keep digital and physical copies
- Photograph documents with your phone as backup

### 5. Track Your IUA Progress
- Know your IUA amount ($1,250, $2,500, or $5,000)
- Calculate remaining IUA throughout the year
- Resets annually on your renewal date

### 6. Submit Regularly
- Don't wait to submit multiple expenses
- Submit within 180 days of service date
- Regular submissions = faster processing

### 7. Pre-Notify Major Procedures
- Contact MPB Health at least 2 weeks before
- Required for procedures over $5,000
- Helps confirm eligibility and negotiate rates

## Submission Process

### Step 1: Gather Documentation
- Complete expense tracker entries
- Collect all supporting documents
- Verify all information is accurate

### Step 2: Access Member Portal
- Log in to your MPB Health member portal
- Navigate to "Submit Medical Bills"
- Click "New Submission"

### Step 3: Upload Documents
- Upload itemized bills (PDF or image)
- Upload proof of payment
- Upload any medical records
- Attach completed expense tracker

### Step 4: Complete Online Form
- Enter provider information
- Specify services received
- Indicate amounts and dates
- Add any special notes

### Step 5: Submit and Track
- Review all information before submitting
- Note your submission confirmation number
- Track status in member portal
- Respond promptly to any requests for additional info

## Timeline Expectations

- **Initial Review:** 2-3 business days
- **Additional Info Requested:** 5-7 business days for member response
- **Approval Decision:** 7-10 business days from complete submission
- **Payment Processing:** 30-45 days from approval
- **Total Timeline:** Typically 30-60 days from submission to payment

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue: Missing Documentation
**Solution:** Keep copies of all bills and receipts. Request itemized bills immediately after service.

### Issue: Late Submission
**Solution:** Submit within 180 days of service. Set calendar reminders to submit monthly.

### Issue: IUA Confusion
**Solution:** Track IUA separately. Only expenses after meeting IUA are shareable.

### Issue: Pre-Authorization Not Obtained
**Solution:** Always call before major procedures. Emergency care is handled differently.

### Issue: Bills Denied
**Solution:** Review sharing guidelines. Appeal if you believe the decision was incorrect.
